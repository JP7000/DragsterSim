Rolling Resistance
▶
Rolling resistance (μr) captures energy lost as wheels deform against the track. F_roll = μr × m × g. Smooth aluminium gives lowest values.
Dimensionless rolling resistance. Typical CO₂ dragster range: 0.006–0.025.
F_roll = μr × m(t) × g — updates each step as CO₂ mass depletes.

What's Slowing Your Car?
At launch — air drag is zero. Resistance is entirely wheel spin-up inertia: F = m_rot_eff × a_launch, where m_rot_eff = Σ(I / r²).

At peak speed — air drag dominates, growing with v². This is the main force limiting your top speed.
